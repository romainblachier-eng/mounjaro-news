Ce médicament injectable, dont le principe actif est la tirzépatide, fonctionne selon un mécanisme double, ciblant deux hormones régulatrices de l’appétit plutôt qu’une seule. Son efficacité supérieure doit cependant être pesée contre son coût et l’accès variable selon les pays.
🇬🇧 English version
Mounjaro may outperform competing medications in the battle against excess weight. This injectable drug, whose active ingredient is tirzepatide, works through a dual mechanism, targeting two appetite-regulating hormones rather than one. Unlike other treatments such as semaglutide, tirzepatide stimulates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors, two biological messengers controlling hunger and satiety. Clinical studies show that patients treated with Mounjaro experience greater weight loss, sometimes up to 20% of initial body weight after one year. The medication is administered once weekly via subcutaneous injection. Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, and digestive disorders, typically temporary. Approved by health authorities, Mounjaro represents a new therapeutic option for people with obesity or excess weight with metabolic complications. Its superior effectiveness must be weighed against its cost and variable access depending on the country.
